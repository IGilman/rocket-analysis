Lessons for Engineering Leaders

4 Takeaways from Rocket's AI Adoption

  • 1. Guardrails must evolve as fast as the AI Every time AI pushed velocity higher, they discovered new edge cases the tooling needed to handle. Static rules get outpaced. The standards and the AI scaled together in a continuous loop.
  • 2. Context is the real multiplier A legible codebase matters more than a capable model. Every annotation written once is read thousands of times. Invest in making your codebase navigable — the returns compound.
  • 3. Treat AI like a contributor, not a tool Holding AI to an identical quality bar prevents the two-tier codebase problem and forces you to build enforcement that benefits every contributor.
  • 4. Productivity gains are only durable when quality is mechanized A velocity spike that degrades the codebase is not a gain — it is a loan with interest. They used 7 custom ESLint plugins, strict TypeScript, 513+ test files, and automated observability to keep the bar mechanical.
